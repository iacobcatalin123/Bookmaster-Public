Copper and Copper Mining

Copper

Copper is a chemical element with the symbol Cu (from Latin: cuprum) and atomic number 29. It is a soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange color.

Copper is an essential element for all living organisms as it is a key constituent of cytochrome c oxidase, complex IV in the mitochondrial respiratory chain. In humans, copper is found mainly in the liver, muscle, and bone.

Copper Mining

Copper mining is the extraction of copper from the earth's crust. The main ores of copper are sulfides, oxides, and carbonates.

Copper ore is usually mined from open pits. The two main types of copper ore are the copper oxide ores and copper sulfide ores. Both types of ore are mined, whether it's through open-pit mining or underground mining.

The extracted copper ore is processed to remove the valuable copper content. The most common methods of extraction include leaching and smelting. The processed copper is then used in a wide variety of products, from electrical wiring to plumbing to electronics.
